      Coral Reef Restoration Campaign

      Aim to restore the health and biodiversity of coral reef ecosystem through coral farming, transplantation and monitoring.

      This campaign focuses on restoring the health and biodiversity of coral reefs through a series of key activities:


      1. Coral Farming: The campaign aims to cultivate healthy coral colonies in nurseries that are later transplant onto degraded reefs. This helps in improving marine lives habitat as well as to rebuild reef structures.

      2. Transplantation: Once corals mature, they are carefully transplanted onto damaged sections of the reef. This process helps jumpstart the recovery of the reef, allowing it to once again support marine life and contribute to the overall biodiversity of the region.

      3. Monitoring: Continuous monitoring of coral reefs ensures the long-term success of restoration efforts.

      By regular assessment the health of transplanted corals and the broader ecosystem, scientists and volunteers can adjust methods and improve future restoration projects.

      In Mauritius, where tourism and fishing heavily depend on the health of coral reefs, restoring these ecosystems is not just an environmental concern but also an economic one. Campaigns like this are essential for protecting the island’s natural beauty, supporting sustainable tourism, and ensuring food security for communities that rely on reef ecosystems.
